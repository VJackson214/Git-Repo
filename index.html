Don Cheadle

Don Cheadle is an acclaimed American actor, producer, and director, celebrated for his versatility and impactful performances. Born on November 29, 1964, in Kansas City, Missouri, he grew up in a military family, moving frequently before settling in Los Angeles. Cheadle attended the California Institute of the Arts, where he developed his passion for acting.

Cheadle’s career took off in the 1990s with notable roles in films like “Devil in a Blue Dress” (1995), but he gained widespread recognition for his performance in “Hotel Rwanda” (2004), where he portrayed Paul Rusesabagina, a hotel manager who sheltered refugees during the Rwandan genocide. This role earned him an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor, showcasing his ability to tackle socially relevant themes.

In addition to his film work, Cheadle is known for his role as James "Rhodey" Rhodes in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, appearing in films like “Iron Man” and “Avengers.” His contributions extend beyond acting; he made his directorial debut with “Miles Ahead” (2015), a biopic about jazz musician Miles Davis, which he also starred in.

Cheadle is also an advocate for humanitarian causes, co-founding the organization Not On Our Watch to address issues like genocide and human rights abuses. With numerous awards to his name, including Golden Globes and Screen Actors Guild Awards, Cheadle remains a prominent figure in both the entertainment industry and social activism.
